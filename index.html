Most recent work

ML Assisted Diagnosis of Heart Disease Risk Factors

I developed a machine learning-based model to predict the risk of heart disease in patients, emphasizing its practical application for early diagnosis. The project involved integrating multiple machine learning algorithms, including decision trees, random forests, and Gradient Boosting, to ensure accurate risk prediction across diverse patient demographics. By optimizing the model's parameters, I achieved a prediction accuracy of 97.7%, enabling clinicians to monitor patient risk factors more effectively and make informed decisions for timely intervention. .

Multi-Epitope Subunit Vaccine Construct Targeting Respiratory Syncytial Virus (RSV)

To address the global health burden posed by Respiratory Syncytial Virus (RSV). I designed a novel epitope-based vaccine to combat Respiratory Syncytial Virus (RSV) using advanced immunoinformatics techniques. The Fusion (F) and Nucleoprotein (N) were identified as optimal vaccine candidates based on their immunogenic potential. I engineered T-cell and B-cell epitopes with minimal human protein homology to reduce cross-reactivity and adverse effects. A 253-residue peptide construct, incorporating a beta-defensin adjuvant, was validated through molecular docking and molecular dynamics simulations. This project demonstrates the potential of computational biology in developing effective solutions for RSV infections.

Deep Learning-Based Diagnostic System for Citrus Plant Diseases

I worked on developing a deep learning model to diagnose citrus plant diseases automatically in orchard settings. For this, I designed a 7-layer convolutional neural network (CNN) that could identify diseases such as greening (HLB), canker, sunscald, and leaf minor. The model was tested on raw images taken directly from the orchards and achieved 98% accuracy in correctly identifying the diseases. This project demonstrates how AI can be applied to improve agricultural practices by making disease detection faster and more reliable. Contributions are welcome!

Multi-Class Brain Tumors Neurological Disorder Stratifying Algorithm

In this project, a deep ensemble model was employed using a transfer learning technique to effectively classify different types of brain tumors, including meningioma, pituitary, and glioma, from MRI images. To achieve this, a robust deep convolutional architecture was built by combining two pre-trained models, VGG19 and Xception, through a concatenation approach. This innovative algorithm outperformed existing methods, demonstrating superior accuracy in identifying brain tumor types. By providing reliable and early tumor type classification, the solution offers significant support for healthcare professionals, assisting them in making informed clinical decisions. This project serves as a valuable contribution to the healthcare field, helping improve the precision of brain tumor diagnoses.
